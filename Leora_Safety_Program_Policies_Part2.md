## 9. INJURY AND ILLNESS REPORTING

### Purpose

To ensure all work-related injuries and illnesses are reported promptly, allowing for proper medical care, investigation, and prevention of future incidents.

### Policy Statement

All employees must report work-related injuries and illnesses immediately, regardless of severity. Failure to report may result in denial of workers compensation benefits and disciplinary action.

**NO RETALIATION:** Employees will never be disciplined, retaliated against, or treated unfavorably for reporting injuries or safety concerns. Reporting injuries is a protected right.

### What Must Be Reported

**Report ALL:**
- Injuries (cuts, bruises, strains, falls, needle sticks, etc.)
- Illnesses believed to be work-related
- Exposures to blood or body fluids
- Near-miss incidents (could have caused injury)
- Unsafe conditions
- Equipment malfunctions
- Property damage

**Even if:**
- Injury seems minor
- You don't think you need medical care
- You're not sure if it's work-related
- You're worried about getting in trouble

**When in doubt, report it.** It's better to report and not need care than to have unreported injury worsen.

### Reporting Timeline

**IMMEDIATE (Same Day):**
- Any injury requiring medical attention
- Blood or body fluid exposure
- Serious injury (fracture, laceration, head injury, etc.)
- Back injury from patient handling
- Motor vehicle accident
- Violence or assault
- Any incident where you feel injured

**WITHIN 24 HOURS:**
- Minor injuries (small cuts, bruises)
- Strains or soreness that develops after work activity
- Near-miss incidents
- Unsafe conditions

**Why Prompt Reporting Matters:**
- Ensures you get needed medical care
- Protects your workers compensation rights
- Allows proper investigation
- Prevents injury to others
- Required by law

**Delayed Reporting:** If injury is not reported within 30 days, workers compensation claim may be denied per Texas law.

### How to Report

**Step 1: Stop and Assess**
- Stop work activity if injured
- Assess severity
- Provide first aid if needed
- Seek emergency care if serious (call 911)

**Step 2: Notify Supervisor Immediately**
- Call supervisor by phone (do not wait)
- Provide details:
  - What happened
  - When it happened (date and time)
  - Where it happened (client home address)
  - What you were doing
  - What body part(s) injured
  - Witnesses present
  - If medical care needed

**Supervisor Contact:**
- During business hours: [Name] at [Phone]
- After hours: [Name] at [Phone]
- If supervisor unavailable: [Alternate] at [Phone]

**Step 3: Complete Incident Report Form**
- Obtain form from supervisor or [location]
- Complete fully and legibly
- Submit within 24 hours
- Keep copy for your records

**Step 4: Seek Medical Care**
- Follow supervisor's instructions
- Go to designated medical provider if non-emergency
- Call 911 or go to ER if emergency
- Take Incident Report Form with you
- Tell medical provider injury is work-related
- Follow all medical advice and treatment
- Keep all appointments
- Get written work status from doctor

### Types of Reportable Incidents

#### Injuries

**Musculoskeletal:**
- Back injuries or strains
- Shoulder injuries
- Neck injuries
- Knee or leg injuries
- Foot or ankle injuries
- Repetitive motion injuries

**Common Causes:**
- Patient lifting or transferring
- Slip, trip, or fall
- Motor vehicle accident
- Repetitive tasks

**Acute Injuries:**
- Cuts and lacerations
- Bruises and contusions
- Burns
- Fractures
- Dislocations
- Head injuries
- Eye injuries

**Common Causes:**
- Sharp objects
- Patient aggression
- Falls
- Motor vehicle accident
- Equipment malfunction

#### Exposures

**Bloodborne Pathogen Exposure:**
- Needle stick or sharps injury
- Splash to eyes, nose, or mouth
- Contact with non-intact skin

**Infectious Disease Exposure:**
- Tuberculosis
- COVID-19 or other respiratory illnesses
- MRSA or other drug-resistant organisms
- Scabies, lice, bedbugs

**Chemical Exposure:**
- Cleaning products
- Medications
- Other chemicals in client homes

#### Illnesses

**Possibly Work-Related Illnesses:**
- Respiratory illness after client exposure
- Skin conditions from work exposures
- Communicable diseases contracted from patients

**Must Report If:**
- You believe illness is related to work
- Exposure occurred at work
- Multiple employees affected (may indicate common source)

#### Near-Miss Incidents

**Definition:** An incident that could have caused injury but did not.

**Examples:**
- Nearly dropped patient during transfer
- Slipped but caught yourself
- Nearly stuck by needle
- Aggressive pet nearly bit you
- Nearly fell on icy steps

**Why Report:**
- Identifies hazards before injury occurs
- Allows corrective action
- Prevents injury to you and coworkers

**No Penalty:** Near-miss reporting is encouraged and will never result in discipline.

### Supervisor Response to Report

**Supervisor Must:**
1. **Listen and Document**
   - Take report seriously
   - Document all details
   - Do not minimize or dismiss

2. **Ensure Medical Care**
   - Arrange immediate care if needed
   - Provide workers comp information
   - Explain designated provider network
   - Authorize emergency care if needed

3. **Report to Insurance Carrier**
   - Contact workers comp carrier within 24 hours
   - Provide all required information
   - Obtain claim number

4. **Investigate**
   - Visit scene if possible
   - Interview witnesses
   - Identify root cause
   - Determine corrective actions

5. **Follow Up**
   - Stay in contact with injured employee
   - Monitor medical treatment
   - Coordinate return to work
   - Implement corrective actions

### First Aid

**First Aid Supplies:**
- First aid kit maintained in office
- Employees should carry basic supplies in car:
  - Band-aids
  - Gauze pads
  - Antiseptic wipes
  - Disposable gloves
  - CPR barrier device

**Common First Aid:**
- Minor cuts: Wash, apply pressure, bandage
- Bruises: Apply cold pack
- Strains: Rest, ice, elevate
- Burns: Cool with water, cover

**When First Aid is NOT Enough:**

**Seek Medical Care for:**
- Deep cuts or lacerations (may need stitches)
- Cuts that won't stop bleeding
- Fractures or suspected fractures
- Head injuries with loss of consciousness, confusion, or severe pain
- Chest pain
- Difficulty breathing
- Severe back or neck injury
- Any injury you're concerned about
- Blood or body fluid exposure (ANY needlestick or splash)

### Medical Treatment

**Workers Compensation Medical Care:**

**Designated Provider:**
- For non-emergency injuries, you must see designated workers comp provider
- Supervisor will provide provider information and authorization
- Your health insurance is NOT used for work injuries
- All work injury care is covered by workers compensation

**Provider Network:**
- [Insurance Carrier] uses [Network Name]
- List of providers available from supervisor
- You must use network providers for care to be covered

**Emergency Care:**
- For true emergencies, go to nearest ER or call 911
- Notify supervisor as soon as possible
- Tell ER injury is work-related
- Follow up with designated provider after ER treatment

**What to Bring to Medical Appointment:**
- Incident Report Form
- Workers compensation information (from supervisor)
- Claim number (if available)
- Photo ID

**At Medical Appointment:**
- Tell provider injury is work-related
- Describe exactly how injury occurred
- Describe your job duties accurately
- Answer all questions honestly
- Follow treatment plan
- Get written work status before leaving:
  - Released to full duty
  - Released to modified duty (with restrictions)
  - Off work
- Schedule follow-up appointment if needed
- Get prescriptions filled (covered by workers comp)

**Follow-Up Care:**
- Attend all scheduled appointments
- Follow medical advice and restrictions
- Keep supervisor informed of status
- Provide work status updates to supervisor
- Continue treatment until released by doctor

**Changing Doctors:**
- You may be able to change doctors within network
- Contact workers comp carrier for approval
- Reasons: not improving, want second opinion, location inconvenient

### Work Status

**Full Duty:**
- Released to work without restrictions
- Can perform all regular job duties
- Notify supervisor of release

**Modified Duty:**
- Released to work with restrictions
- Restrictions examples:
  - No lifting over X pounds
  - No bending or twisting
  - No driving
  - Reduced hours
- Provide written restrictions to supervisor immediately
- Supervisor will determine if modified work available
- Follow restrictions exactly (no exceptions)

**Off Work:**
- Doctor determines you cannot work
- Provide off-work note to supervisor
- May receive disability payments through workers comp
- Keep scheduled medical appointments
- Notify supervisor of any status change
- Do not perform activities inconsistent with off-work status

### Workers Compensation Benefits

**If Work Injury Prevents You from Working:**

**Medical Benefits:**
- All reasonable and necessary medical treatment covered
- No co-pays or deductibles for you
- Mileage reimbursement for medical appointments

**Disability Benefits (if off work 7+ days):**
- Temporary Income Benefits (TIBs)
- 70% of average weekly wage (up to state maximum)
- Starts after 7-day waiting period
- Payable until you return to work or reach maximum medical improvement
- Check with workers comp carrier for amount and payment schedule

**Important:**
- Must be under doctor's care to receive benefits
- Must attend all appointments
- Cannot work elsewhere while receiving disability benefits
- Report any earnings to workers comp carrier

### Investigation

**Purpose of Investigation:**
- Determine what happened
- Identify root cause
- Prevent similar incidents
- Not to assign blame or discipline

**Investigation Process:**
1. **Scene Examination**
   - Visit location if possible
   - Photograph if appropriate
   - Identify hazards

2. **Interviews**
   - Injured employee
   - Witnesses
   - Client/family (if appropriate)

3. **Analysis**
   - What happened (sequence of events)
   - Why it happened (root cause)
   - Contributing factors

4. **Corrective Actions**
   - Immediate corrections
   - Long-term solutions
   - Training needs
   - Policy changes
   - Equipment needs

5. **Documentation**
   - Investigation report
   - Witness statements
   - Photos
   - Corrective action plan

6. **Follow-Up**
   - Implement corrective actions
   - Verify effectiveness
   - Communicate to all staff if lesson learned

**Employee Cooperation:**
- You will be asked to provide details
- Answer all questions honestly
- Purpose is prevention, not blame
- Your input is valuable

### Privacy and Confidentiality

**Medical Information:**
- Your medical information is confidential
- Only shared with:
  - Supervisor (need to know for work status)
  - Workers comp carrier
  - Medical providers
  - Required by law
- Not shared with coworkers without your permission

**Incident Reports:**
- Treated as confidential
- Shared only with those who need to know
- Your name may be removed when sharing lessons learned

### Return to Work

**Goal:** Safe return to work as soon as medically cleared.

**Modified Duty Program:**
- We want you back at work even if you can't perform full duties
- Modified work available if possible within your restrictions
- May include:
  - Office work
  - Training development
  - Light duty tasks
  - Reduced hours
- Allows you to:
  - Maintain connection to workplace
  - Continue earning full wages
  - Heal while remaining productive
  - Transition gradually back to full duty

**Return-to-Work Process:**
1. Doctor releases you to modified or full duty
2. Provide written work status to supervisor
3. Supervisor determines available work within restrictions
4. Return to work on agreed date
5. Supervisor monitors to ensure restrictions followed
6. Continue medical treatment until full release
7. Gradually transition to full duties as medically appropriate

### Rights and Responsibilities

**Your Rights:**
- Right to report injuries without retaliation
- Right to receive medical care
- Right to workers compensation benefits if eligible
- Right to a safe workplace
- Right to refuse unsafe work

**Your Responsibilities:**
- Report all injuries promptly and honestly
- Seek medical care when needed
- Follow medical advice and treatment plans
- Attend all medical appointments
- Keep supervisor informed of status
- Provide work status updates
- Return to work when medically cleared
- Follow work restrictions exactly
- Cooperate with investigation

**Prohibited:**
- Failing to report injury
- Providing false information
- Exaggerating injury
- Working outside restrictions
- Missing medical appointments without notification
- Refusing light duty within restrictions (may affect benefits)
- Filing fraudulent claim

### Recordkeeping

**Records Maintained:**
- Incident Report Forms
- Investigation Reports
- Medical documentation (work status, restrictions)
- Workers compensation claim information
- Return-to-work documentation

**Retention:**
- Injury records: 5 years minimum
- Exposure records: Duration of employment + 30 years

## 10. EMERGENCY RESPONSE PROCEDURES

### Purpose

To prepare employees to respond effectively to emergencies encountered during work activities.

### Scope

Emergencies may occur in client homes, during travel, or in the office.

### Emergency Contacts

**Emergency Services:**
- **Fire, Police, Ambulance: 911**

**Company Contacts:**
- Supervisor: [Name] - [Phone]
- After-Hours Emergency: [Name] - [Phone]
- Safety Coordinator: [Name] - [Phone]

**Poison Control:**
- National Poison Control: 1-800-222-1222

**Utility Emergencies:**
- Gas Leak: [Local gas company emergency number]
- Power Outage: [Local electric company number]

### Medical Emergencies

**Patient Medical Emergency:**

**Signs of Emergency:**
- Unresponsive or decreased consciousness
- Difficulty breathing or not breathing
- Chest pain
- Severe bleeding
- Signs of stroke (sudden weakness, drooping face, speech difficulty)
- Severe pain
- Seizure
- Choking

**Your Response:**
1. **Call 911 immediately** - Do not delay
2. Stay with patient if safe to do so
3. Provide first aid if trained and able:
   - CPR if not breathing (use barrier device)
   - Control bleeding (apply pressure, use gloves)
   - Position patient safely
   - Do not move patient unless immediate danger
4. Notify family/emergency contact
5. Notify supervisor
6. Provide information to paramedics when they arrive:
   - Patient's name and age
   - Medical history (if known)
   - Medications patient takes
   - What happened
   - Care you provided
7. Document incident thoroughly

**Employee Medical Emergency:**

If you experience medical emergency while working:
1. Call 911 if needed
2. Notify supervisor
3. Get to safe location
4. Alert client/family if appropriate
5. Seek medical care

**Do Not:**
- Give patient medications unless you are authorized and it's part of care plan
- Move patient with suspected spinal injury
- Give anything by mouth to unconscious person
- Leave patient alone unless you must leave to call 911 and no one else present

### Fire Emergencies

**If Fire in Client Home:**

**RACE Protocol:**
- **R**escue - Remove patient from immediate danger if safe
- **A**larm - Call 911, alert others
- **C**ontain - Close doors to contain fire if possible
- **E**vacuate - Get out of building, help patient evacuate if possible

**Procedure:**
1. If small fire and you know how to use fire extinguisher safely, may attempt to extinguish
2. If fire is large, growing, or you're unsure - GET OUT
3. Call 911 once safely outside
4. Do not re-enter building
5. Meet fire department and provide information
6. Notify supervisor
7. Notify patient's family

**Fire Extinguisher Use (PASS):**
- **P**ull pin
- **A**im at base of fire
- **S**queeze handle
- **S**weep side to side

**Only fight fire if:**
- Fire is small and contained
- You have clear exit behind you
- You know how to use extinguisher
- Fire is not between you and exit

**When to Evacuate Immediately:**
- Fire is large or spreading
- Smoke is thick
- Fire is blocking exit
- You feel unsafe

**Evacuating Patient:**
- Assist ambulatory patient to walk out quickly
- Use wheelchair if available and time permits
- If patient cannot walk and no wheelchair:
  - Drag using blanket or sheet if absolutely necessary
  - Protect head and spine
  - Only if immediate life threat and no other option
- Your safety first - do not risk your life

### Severe Weather

**Tornado Warning:**

If tornado warning issued while in client home:
1. Move to interior room on lowest floor
2. Away from windows
3. Bathrooms, closets, hallways ideal
4. Get under sturdy furniture if available
5. Cover head and neck
6. Help patient to safe location
7. Stay until all-clear given
8. Call 911 if injuries
9. Notify supervisor when safe

**If Tornado Warning While Driving:**
- Never try to outrun tornado
- If possible, get to sturdy shelter
- If no shelter:
  - Park safely away from traffic
  - Stay in vehicle with seatbelt on
  - Duck below windows, cover head
- Do not take shelter under overpass (dangerous)

**Severe Thunderstorm:**
- Stay indoors if possible
- Avoid windows
- Do not use corded phones
- Unplug sensitive equipment
- If caught outside, avoid open areas, tall trees, water
- Get inside vehicle if no building available

**Flooding:**
- Never drive through flooded roadways
- Turn around, don't drown
- If trapped in rising water, call 911
- Get to higher ground
- Do not enter flooded client homes (electrical hazard)

**Winter Storm/Ice:**
- If roads unsafe, do not travel (notify supervisor)
- If caught in storm:
  - Pull off road safely
  - Stay in vehicle
  - Run engine 10 minutes per hour for heat
  - Keep exhaust pipe clear of snow
  - Call for help
- In client home during storm:
  - May need to stay until roads safe
  - Notify supervisor and family

### Utility Emergencies

**Natural Gas Leak:**

**Signs:**
- Smell of rotten eggs or sulfur
- Hissing sound
- Dirt blowing in air

**If Gas Leak Suspected:**
1. Evacuate immediately - do not turn on/off anything
2. Do not use phone, light switches, or anything that could spark
3. Get everyone out of building
4. Call gas company and 911 from outside location
5. Do not re-enter
6. Notify supervisor

**Power Outage:**
- Check if neighbors also affected (local outage vs. home issue)
- Report to utility company
- Patient safety considerations:
  - Patients on electrically powered medical equipment (ventilator, oxygen concentrator)
  - Refrigerated medications
  - May need to arrange backup power or relocation
- Notify supervisor immediately if patient equipment affected

**Water Leak/Flooding:**
- Shut off water if safe and you know how
- Move patient away from water
- Call supervisor
- May need to relocate patient
- Do not walk through standing water (electrical hazard)

### Carbon Monoxide

**Signs of Carbon Monoxide Poisoning:**
- Headache
- Dizziness
- Nausea
- Confusion
- Loss of consciousness

**If Suspected:**
1. Get everyone outside immediately
2. Call 911
3. Do not re-enter
4. Ventilate building (fire department will do this)
5. Seek medical evaluation for anyone symptomatic
6. Do not use gas appliances until inspected

### Violence and Active Threat

**Active Shooter or Violent Intruder:**

**RUN - HIDE - FIGHT:**

**RUN:**
- If safe exit route exists, run
- Leave belongings
- Help others if possible but don't slow yourself
- Call 911 when safe

**HIDE:**
- If you cannot run, hide
- Lock and barricade door
- Turn off lights
- Silence phone
- Hide out of view
- Stay quiet
- Call 911 if possible

**FIGHT:**
- Only as last resort when in immediate danger
- Use anything as weapon
- Be aggressive
- Commit to action

**When Police Arrive:**
- Keep hands visible
- Follow commands immediately
- Do not run toward officers
- Provide information about shooter/threat

**After Incident:**
- Seek medical care for injuries
- Provide information to police
- Notify supervisor
- Seek counseling/support

### Hazardous Material Spills

**In Client Home:**

Small spill (gloves needed):
- Stop spread
- Clean up using proper PPE
- Dispose properly
- Wash hands

Large spill or unknown chemical:
- Evacuate area
- Call 911
- Do not attempt cleanup
- Notify supervisor

### Missing or Wandering Patient

**If Patient with Dementia is Missing:**
1. Search immediate area (rooms, closets, outside)
2. Check with neighbors
3. If not found within 15 minutes, call 911
4. Notify family
5. Notify supervisor
6. Provide description and recent photo to police
7. Check common places patient might go

**Prevention:**
- Keep doors locked
- Use alarms on doors if available
- Ensure patient has ID bracelet
- Keep recent photo available

### Chemical Exposure

**If You or Patient Exposed to Chemical:**

**Eye Contact:**
- Rinse with water immediately for 15 minutes
- Hold eyelid open
- Remove contact lenses
- Seek medical care
- Bring product label/Safety Data Sheet if available

**Skin Contact:**
- Remove contaminated clothing
- Rinse skin with water for 15 minutes
- Wash with soap and water
- Seek medical care if irritation persists
- Bring product label

**Inhalation:**
- Get to fresh air immediately
- Call 911 if breathing difficulty
- Loosen tight clothing
- Seek medical care

**Ingestion:**
- Call Poison Control: 1-800-222-1222
- Follow their instructions
- Do not induce vomiting unless instructed
- Have product container/label available

### Heat-Related Illness

**Heat Exhaustion:**

**Signs:**
- Heavy sweating
- Weakness
- Nausea
- Headache
- Dizziness

**Treatment:**
- Move to cool location
- Loosen clothing
- Apply cool water or compresses
- Sip water
- Rest
- Seek medical care if not improving

**Heat Stroke (EMERGENCY):**

**Signs:**
- High body temperature (>103Â°F)
- Hot, dry skin or heavy sweating
- Confusion
- Loss of consciousness
- Seizure

**Treatment:**
- Call 911 immediately
- Move to cool location
- Remove outer clothing
- Cool with water
- Do not give fluids if unconscious

**Prevention:**
- Stay hydrated
- Take breaks in air conditioning
- Avoid heavy exertion in heat
- Wear light clothing

### Choking

**If Patient is Choking:**

**Signs:**
- Cannot talk or breathe
- Clutching throat
- Skin turning blue

**For Conscious Adult:**
1. Ask "Are you choking?"
2. If yes and cannot breathe:
3. Perform Heimlich maneuver (abdominal thrusts):
   - Stand behind patient
   - Place fist above navel
   - Grasp fist with other hand
   - Give quick upward thrusts
   - Repeat until object expelled or patient unconscious
4. Call 911 if not resolving

**If Patient Becomes Unconscious:**
- Call 911
- Lower to floor
- Begin CPR
- Check mouth for object before giving breaths

### Bloodborne Pathogen Exposure

**If You Experience Needlestick or Blood/Body Fluid Exposure:**
1. **Wash immediately:**
   - Needlestick: Wash with soap and water
   - Eyes: Flush with water for 15 minutes
   - Mouth: Rinse with water
2. **Report to supervisor immediately** - do not delay
3. **Seek medical care within 2 hours** (critical for post-exposure prophylaxis)
4. **Complete incident report**

**See full Bloodborne Pathogen policy (Section 5) for details.**

### Documentation

**After Any Emergency:**
- Complete incident report
- Document actions taken
- Note time of emergency and response
- List witnesses
- Describe outcome
- Submit to supervisor within 24 hours

### Training

**All employees will receive training on:**
- Emergency procedures
- Emergency contact numbers
- How to call 911
- Fire safety and evacuation
- Severe weather response
- CPR and First Aid (if applicable to role)

**Annual Review:**
- Emergency procedures reviewed annually
- Updates communicated promptly
- Practice drills encouraged

## 11. DRUG-FREE WORKPLACE POLICY

### Purpose

To provide a safe, healthy, and productive work environment free from the effects of alcohol and drugs.

### Policy Statement

Leora Home Health is committed to maintaining a drug-free workplace. The unlawful manufacture, distribution, dispensing, possession, or use of controlled substances or alcohol in the workplace is strictly prohibited.

This policy complies with the Drug-Free Workplace Act and may qualify the company for workers compensation premium discounts under Texas law.

### Scope

This policy applies to:
- All employees (full-time, part-time, temporary)
- During work hours
- On company premises
- In company vehicles
- At client homes
- During company business or events

### Prohibited Substances

**Prohibited:**
- Illegal drugs (marijuana, cocaine, heroin, methamphetamine, etc.)
- Controlled substances without valid prescription
- Alcohol
- Synthetic drugs (synthetic marijuana, bath salts, etc.)
- Inhalants used for intoxication
- Prescription drugs used other than prescribed
- Any substance that impairs ability to work safely

**Note:** Marijuana is illegal under federal law and prohibited by this policy, even if legal under state law. Texas has not legalized recreational marijuana.

### Prohibited Conduct

**Employees May Not:**
- Use, possess, manufacture, or distribute prohibited substances during work or on work premises
- Report to work under the influence of prohibited substances
- Have detectable amounts of prohibited substances in their system during work
- Consume alcohol during work hours
- Possess alcohol during work hours or on work premises
- Use prescription drugs in a manner not prescribed or in a way that impairs work performance
- Refuse to submit to drug/alcohol testing when required
- Attempt to tamper with, substitute, or adulterate drug test samples

### Prescription and Over-the-Counter Medications

**Allowed:**
- Employees may use lawfully prescribed medications and over-the-counter drugs as directed

**Requirements:**
- Must be prescribed to you by licensed physician
- Used as prescribed
- Does not impair ability to perform job safely

**Reporting:**
- If prescription medication may affect your ability to work safely, notify supervisor
- Examples: Causes drowsiness, affects coordination, impairs judgment
- Information kept confidential
- Company will determine if accommodation needed (modified duty, temporary reassignment)

**Do Not:**
- Share prescription medications with others
- Use someone else's prescription
- Use more than prescribed
- Mix medications with alcohol

### Medical Marijuana

**Policy:** Medical marijuana, even with valid prescription, is prohibited.

**Reason:**
- Marijuana remains illegal under federal law
- Healthcare is federally regulated industry
- Patient safety requires drug-free caregivers
- Workers compensation issues

**Consequence:** Use of medical marijuana, even with prescription, violates this policy and may result in termination.

**Note:** This policy will be reviewed if federal law changes.

### Drug and Alcohol Testing

**Testing Circumstances:**

**1. Pre-Employment Testing:**
- All final applicants tested before employment
- Job offer conditional on passing drug test
- Refusal to test results in no hire

**2. Reasonable Suspicion Testing:**
- When supervisor has reasonable belief employee is under the influence
- Based on observable signs:
  - Slurred speech
  - Unsteady gait
  - Odor of alcohol or drugs
  - Bloodshot eyes
  - Erratic behavior
  - Impaired coordination
  - Unusual behavior
- Must be documented by supervisor
- Testing conducted immediately

**3. Post-Accident Testing:**
- Required after work-related accident or injury
- If accident resulted in:
  - Injury requiring medical treatment beyond first aid
  - Property damage over $[amount]
  - Vehicle accident
  - Near-miss with potential for serious injury
- Testing conducted within 2-4 hours if possible, within 8 hours maximum

**4. Return-to-Duty Testing:**
- Required before returning to work after:
  - Policy violation
  - Completion of treatment program
- Must have negative test before resuming work

**5. Follow-Up Testing:**
- Required after return to duty following policy violation
- Unannounced tests
- Minimum [6-12] tests over [12-24] months
- As determined by substance abuse professional

**6. Random Testing (Optional):**
- May be implemented if deemed necessary
- All employees subject to selection
- Selection by computer-generated random method
- No advance notice

### Testing Procedures

**Drug Test:**
- Urine test at certified laboratory
- Chain of custody procedures
- Tests for:
  - Marijuana (THC)
  - Cocaine
  - Amphetamines/Methamphetamine
  - Opiates/Opioids
  - Phencyclidine (PCP)
  - Other substances as deemed appropriate
- Split sample collected (for confirmation if needed)

**Alcohol Test:**
- Breath test using evidential breath testing device
- Conducted by trained technician
- 0.04% BAC or greater is positive
- Confirmation test if initial test is 0.02% or greater

**Procedure:**
1. Employee escorted to testing facility or tested on-site
2. Photo ID required
3. Employee completes paperwork
4. Sample collected under observation
5. Chain of custody maintained
6. Sample sent to certified lab
7. Results sent to Medical Review Officer (MRO)
8. MRO reviews results and contacts employee if positive
9. Results reported to employer

**Medical Review Officer (MRO):**
- Licensed physician
- Reviews all test results
- Contacts employee if positive test
- Determines if legitimate medical explanation
- Reports final result to employer

**Refusal to Test:**

Considered refusal if employee:
- Refuses to submit to test
- Fails to provide adequate sample without valid medical reason
- Tampers with sample
- Fails to report to testing facility
- Leaves testing facility before completing test
- Fails to cooperate with testing process

**Consequence:** Refusal = Positive test (termination)

### Confidentiality

**Results:**
- Test results are confidential medical information
- Shared only with:
  - Management (need to know)
  - Medical Review Officer
  - Substance abuse professional (if applicable)
  - As required by law
- Not shared with coworkers
- Maintained in confidential medical file separate from personnel file

### Consequences of Policy Violation

## 13. RETURN-TO-WORK PROGRAM

### Purpose

To facilitate safe, timely return to work for employees who have been absent due to work-related injury or illness.

### Policy Statement

Leora Home Health is committed to returning injured employees to work as soon as medically appropriate. We recognize that staying connected to the workplace promotes healing, maintains job skills, and benefits both employee and employer.

### Benefits of Return-to-Work Program

**For Employees:**
- Continue earning wages (potentially full wages)
- Maintain health benefits
- Stay connected to coworkers and workplace
- Maintain job skills
- Promotes faster recovery
- Reduces financial stress
- Demonstrates value to employer

**For Employer:**
- Reduces workers compensation costs
- Maintains experienced employee
- Demonstrates commitment to employee welfare
- Reduces need for temporary replacement
- Lowers Experience Modification Rate over time

### Program Coordinator

**Return-to-Work Coordinator:** [Name and Title]

**Responsibilities:**
- Communicate with injured employee
- Coordinate with medical providers
- Identify available modified work
- Monitor employee during transitional work
- Adjust duties as employee progresses
- Facilitate communication between all parties

### Communication with Injured Employee

**Employer Will:**
- Contact injured employee within 24 hours of injury
- Maintain regular contact during absence (weekly minimum)
- Express concern for employee well-being
- Keep employee informed about return-to-work options
- Answer questions about benefits and status
- Not pressure employee to return before medically cleared

**Employee Will:**
- Keep employer informed of medical status
- Provide work status updates from doctor promptly
- Attend all medical appointments
- Follow medical treatment plan
- Communicate about recovery progress
- Be open to modified duty options

### Medical Management

**Work Status Documentation:**

Employee must provide written work status from treating physician:

**1. Full Duty Release:**
- Can perform all regular job duties without restrictions
- Returns to regular position and full schedule
- No modified work needed

**2. Modified Duty with Restrictions:**
- Can work with specified restrictions
- Doctor specifies restrictions:
  - No lifting over X pounds
  - No bending, twisting, or stooping
  - No climbing or kneeling
  - No driving
  - No repetitive reaching
  - Reduced hours (specify)
  - Frequent breaks required
  - Other specific limitations
- Employer determines if modified work available within restrictions
- Goal is gradual transition to full duty

**3. Off Work/Disabled:**
- Cannot perform any work
- Employee remains off work
- Receives workers compensation disability benefits if eligible
- Maintains contact with employer
- Provides updated work status at each medical visit

**Updating Work Status:**
- Employee must provide written work status after each medical visit
- If status changes (better or worse), notify employer immediately
- Doctor may update restrictions as employee progresses
- Goal is progression toward full duty

### Modified Duty/Transitional Work

**What is Modified Duty:**
- Temporary work assignment within employee's restrictions
- May or may not be employee's regular duties
- Allows employee to work while still healing
- Transitional step toward full duty return

**Types of Modified Duty:**

**1. Regular Job with Restrictions:**
- Perform usual duties but avoid restricted activities
- Example: Nurse provides patient care but no lifting; family member assists with transfers
- May be possible for some patients

**2. Alternate Patient Assignments:**
- Assign to patients requiring less physical demand
- Example: Patients who are independent with transfers
- May require temporary schedule changes

**3. Light Duty Clinical Work:**
- Case management duties
- Patient assessments (if restrictions allow)
- Patient education
- Care plan development
- Quality assurance calls
- Coordination of care

**4. Administrative Work:**
- Data entry
- Filing
- Scheduling assistance
- Billing support
- Training material development
- Policy and procedure review
- Inventory
- Supply organization

**5. Combination:**
- Part patient care (within restrictions)
- Part administrative duties
- Allows some connection to regular duties

**Modified Duty Requirements:**
- Must be within doctor's restrictions (100% compliance)
- Should be productive and meaningful
- Preferably related to employee's regular work
- May be lower skill level (that's okay for transitional work)
- May be reduced hours if medical restriction
- Employee receives regular wage rate (not reduced pay)

### Modified Duty Process

**Step 1: Receive Work Status**
- Employee provides written restrictions from doctor
- Return-to-Work Coordinator reviews restrictions

**Step 2: Identify Available Modified Work**
- Coordinator reviews work duties available
- Matches available work to employee restrictions
- Creates modified duty plan

**Step 3: Offer Modified Work**
- Coordinator offers modified duty to employee
- Explains duties, schedule, duration
- Answers questions
- Gets employee agreement

**Step 4: Implement Modified Duty**
- Employee starts modified work on agreed date
- Supervisor monitors work and restrictions
- Ensures restrictions are followed
- Provides necessary tools and support

**Step 5: Monitor and Adjust**
- Weekly check-ins with employee
- Monitor recovery progress
- Adjust duties as restrictions change
- Ensure employee not exceeding restrictions
- Address any concerns

**Step 6: Transition to Full Duty**
- As restrictions lift, gradually increase duties
- Return to regular duties as able
- Continue until full duty release from doctor

**Duration:**
- Modified duty is temporary
- Goal is return to full duty
- Duration depends on injury and recovery
- Typically weeks to months
- Reviewed regularly

### Employee Rights and Responsibilities

**Employee Rights:**
- Right to modified work if available within restrictions
- Right to refuse work outside restrictions
- Right to workers compensation benefits
- Right to privacy of medical information
- Right to non-retaliation for injury

**Employee Responsibilities:**
- Provide timely work status from doctor
- Follow restrictions exactly (do not exceed)
- Attend all medical appointments
- Follow treatment plan
- Perform modified duties as assigned
- Communicate concerns or problems
- Act in good faith toward recovery and return to work

**Employee May Not:**
- Refuse modified work within restrictions (may affect workers comp benefits)
- Exceed medical restrictions
- Perform restricted activities
- Miss medical appointments without notice
- Fail to provide updated work status

### Modified Work Not Available

**If No Modified Work Within Restrictions:**
- Employee remains off work
- Receives workers compensation disability benefits if eligible
- Maintains regular contact with employer
- Provides work status updates
- Returns when restrictions allow or full duty release

**Company Will:**
- Make good faith effort to find modified work
- Be creative in identifying duties
- Periodically review as restrictions change
- Document efforts to accommodate

**Company Not Required To:**
- Create non-essential work
- Displace other employees
- Hold position open indefinitely
- Guarantee modified work available

### Workers Compensation Benefits During Modified Duty

**Temporary Income Benefits (TIBs):**
- If modified duty pays less than pre-injury wages, may receive supplemental benefits
- Workers comp carrier pays difference (up to 70% of wage loss)
- Carrier determines eligibility and amount

**Medical Benefits:**
- Continue during modified duty
- All work-injury medical care covered
- Follow treatment plan

**Return to Full Wages:**
- Goal is return to full wages as soon as medically possible
- Modified duty often allows continued full wage payment
- Better for employee than disability benefits

### Employer Accommodations

**Reasonable Accommodations May Include:**
- Ergonomic modifications (chairs, equipment)
- Schedule adjustments (reduced hours, flexible schedule)
- Modified patient assignments
- Assistance with heavy tasks
- Frequent breaks
- Parking accommodations

**Employer Will:**
- Consider accommodations requested by doctor or employee
- Provide accommodations that don't create undue hardship
- Engage in interactive process with employee

### Return to Full Duty

**When Doctor Releases to Full Duty:**
1. Employee provides written full duty release
2. Return-to-Work Coordinator reviews
3. Employee returns to regular position and duties
4. Gradually increase workload if needed (first few days)
5. Monitor for any recurrence or problems
6. Stay in touch with employee to ensure successful transition

**If Employee Has Concerns:**
- Discuss with supervisor
- May need to return to doctor for re-evaluation
- Do not continue working if pain or limitations

### Permanent Restrictions

**If Employee Has Permanent Limitations:**
- After reaching maximum medical improvement
- Doctor releases to full duty with permanent restrictions
- Employer determines if can accommodate
- May require permanent job modification
- ADA may apply
- Vocational rehabilitation may be available through workers comp

### Reinjury Prevention

**Goal:** Prevent reinjury when returning to work

**Strategies:**
- Gradual return (don't overdo)
- Follow all restrictions
- Use proper body mechanics
- Use equipment and ask for help
- Report any pain or problems immediately
- Stretching and conditioning exercises per doctor
- Don't try to "prove yourself" by doing too much

### Program Success Measures

**Indicators of Successful Program:**
- High percentage of injured employees return to work
- Average number of days off work decreases
- Employees satisfied with program
- Workers comp costs decrease over time

**Tracking:**
- Number of work injuries
- Number of lost workdays
- Number of employees on modified duty
- Average duration of modified duty
- Successful transitions to full duty
- Reinjury rates

### Confidentiality

**Medical Information:**
- Employee's medical information is confidential
- Only shared with those who need to know:
  - Return-to-Work Coordinator
  - Direct supervisor
  - Workers comp carrier
- Restrictions shared but not diagnosis
- Medical records kept in confidential file
