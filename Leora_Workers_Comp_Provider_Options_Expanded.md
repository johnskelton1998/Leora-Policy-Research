## EVALUATION GUIDE

### How to Compare Financial Strength Ratings

**A.M. Best Ratings:**
- **A++, A+** (Superior) - Top tier, exceptional financial strength
- **A, A-** (Excellent) - Strong, reliable
- **B++, B+** (Good) - Adequate, but less secure
- Below B+ - Avoid

**What to Look For:**
- Minimum A- rating
- A or higher preferred
- A++ for absolute security (but costs more)

**Why It Matters:**
- Carrier pays your employees' claims
- Weak carrier could become insolvent
- Claims could go unpaid if carrier fails

---

### Questions to Ask Each Provider

**Coverage Questions:**
1. What is included in the base policy?
2. What are the employer's liability limits?
3. Are there any coverage exclusions specific to home health care?
4. What endorsements do you recommend?
5. Is waiver of subrogation available if clients require it?

**Pricing Questions:**
6. What is the exact class code rate per $100 of payroll?
7. Are there any additional fees (policy fees, inspection fees, etc.)?
8. What discounts are available (safety program, drug-free workplace)?
9. What payment plans do you offer?
10. Is there an installment fee for monthly payments?
11. What is the deposit requirement?
12. How is the annual audit conducted?

**Claims Questions:**
13. How do I report a claim (phone, online, app)?
14. What is your average claim closure time?
15. Do you have a medical provider network in the Austin area?
16. Who will be my claims adjuster?
17. Do you provide nurse case management?
18. What is your return-to-work program?

**Service Questions:**
19. Who will be my day-to-day contact?
20. How quickly can you issue certificates of insurance?
21. What loss control services do you provide?
22. Do you offer safety training resources?
23. How does the renewal process work?
24. Will my rates increase if I have a claim?

**Technology Questions:**
25. Do you have an online portal?
26. Can I access policy documents digitally?
27. Do you offer a mobile app?
28. Can I integrate with my payroll system?
29. How do I request certificates of insurance?

---

### Red Flags to Watch For

**Pricing Red Flags:**
- Unusually low quotes (may indicate coverage gaps or carrier issues)
- Hidden fees not disclosed upfront
- Unwillingness to explain rate calculation
- Rates that seem too good to be true

**Service Red Flags:**
- Pressure to bind immediately without time to review
- Unwillingness to provide references
- Poor online reviews (check Google, Better Business Bureau)
- Difficulty getting questions answered
- No clear point of contact
- Vague answers about claims process

**Coverage Red Flags:**
- Exclusions not clearly explained
- Lower employer's liability limits without explanation
- Unwillingness to provide policy sample
- Missing required coverage for your client contracts

**Financial Red Flags:**
- Rating below A-
- Recent rating downgrade
- News of financial troubles
- Not licensed in Texas (verify at TDI.texas.gov)

---

### How to Negotiate Better Rates

**Strategies:**

1. **Safety Program Discount**
   - Implement written safety program BEFORE getting quotes
   - Document training
   - Share with underwriter
   - Can reduce rates 5-15%

2. **Shop Multiple Carriers**
   - Get at least 3-5 quotes
   - Use competition to negotiate
   - Ask if carrier can match or beat competitor

3. **Multi-Policy Discount**
   - Bundle workers comp with general liability
   - Bundle with professional liability
   - Can save 10-20% total

4. **Payment in Full Discount**
   - Pay annual premium upfront if cash flow allows
   - Saves installment fees (typically 5-7%)

5. **Drug-Free Workplace**
   - Implement policy before quote
   - May qualify for state-mandated discount
   - Document and certify to carrier

6. **Claims-Free History**
   - If you have prior coverage with no claims, highlight it
   - Request claims-free discount
   - Provide loss runs as proof

7. **Higher Deductible**
   - Consider $500-$2,500 deductible per claim
   - Can reduce premium 10-25%
   - Only if you have cash reserves

8. **Broker Negotiation**
   - Brokers can sometimes negotiate with carriers
   - Ask broker to push for better rate
   - Brokers want your business and renewals

9. **Timing**
   - Shop 60-90 days before you need coverage
   - Rushing limits negotiation power
   - Carriers more flexible with time

10. **Commitment**
    - Express intention for long-term relationship
    - Carriers value retention
    - May offer better initial rate
