## 3. PATIENT HANDLING & LIFTING

### Key Principles
**NEVER lift alone if:**
- Patient weighs >50 lbs and requires full lift
- Patient unable to bear weight
- Patient has unpredictable behavior
- You feel uncomfortable or unsafe

**ALWAYS:**
- Assess patient's mobility before transfer
- Use mechanical lifts when available
- Request assistance when needed
- Maintain proper body mechanics

### Body Mechanics Essentials
1. **Plan Ahead** - Assess ability, clear path, position equipment
2. **Proper Stance** - Feet shoulder-width, one foot forward, bend knees not waist
3. **During Lift** - Hold patient close, use legs not back, no twisting, pivot feet to turn
4. **Communication** - Explain actions, count "1, 2, 3" before lifting

### Patient Mobility Levels

| Level | Description | Equipment | Technique |
|-------|-------------|-----------|-----------|
| **1: Independent** | No help needed | None | Standby supervision only |
| **2: Minimal Assist** | Light guidance | Gait belt | Light touch, verbal cues |
| **3: Moderate Assist** | Significant support | Gait belt, transfer board | May need 2-person assist |
| **4: Maximum Assist** | Most of work on caregiver | Mechanical lift recommended | **2-person required** |
| **5: Total Assist** | Cannot bear weight | **Mechanical lift required** | **2-person required, DO NOT manual lift** |

### Two-Person Lift Required When:
- Patient >150 lbs needing moderate-to-maximum assistance
- History of falls or unpredictable movements
- Unable to follow directions
- Cramped or awkward space
- Single caregiver feels unsafe

### Prohibited Practices
**NEVER:**
- Lift patient by grasping under arms
- Twist spine while lifting
- Lift patient exceeding your capability
- Rush a transfer
- Use broken equipment

## 4. HOME ENVIRONMENT SAFETY

### Pre-Service Assessment
Complete home safety checklist before first visit:

**Critical Items:**
- [ ] Walkways clear, steps safe with handrails
- [ ] Interior pathways unobstructed, adequate lighting
- [ ] Adequate space around bed/care areas
- [ ] Running water, soap for hand washing
- [ ] Smoke/CO detectors functional
- [ ] Pets can be secured during visit
- [ ] No weapons visible/unsecured
- [ ] Clear exits for emergencies

### Risk Levels

**Low Risk:** Standard precautions adequate

**Moderate Risk:** Additional precautions required
- Family/caregiver present during visits
- Daylight hours only
- Maintain clear exit path
- Check-in/check-out with supervisor

**High Risk:** Special measures required
- **Two-person visits required**
- Limited visit duration
- Consider service termination if risks can't be mitigated

### Weather Policy
Employees **not required** to work when:
- Roads officially closed
- Severe weather warnings active
- Conditions unsafe in employee's judgment

**No discipline for weather-related delays. Employee safety is priority.**

### Right to Refuse Unsafe Work
Employees may refuse service if conditions present imminent danger. Leave premises, contact supervisor, document situation. **No retaliation for good-faith safety refusal.**

## 5. INFECTION CONTROL & BLOODBORNE PATHOGENS

### Standard Precautions
**Treat all blood and body fluids as infectious.**

Applies to: Blood, body fluids (except sweat), non-intact skin, mucous membranes

### Hand Hygiene

**When to Wash:**
- Before and after patient contact
- Before and after wearing gloves
- After contact with blood/body fluids
- Before medication preparation
- After restroom, coughing, sneezing

**Technique:** 20 seconds minimum - all surfaces, under nails, wrists

### PPE Requirements

| Task | Required PPE |
|------|--------------|
| Basic care (no exposure) | None (use judgment) |
| Bathing/hygiene | Gloves |
| Incontinence care | Gloves + gown/apron if heavy fluid |
| Wound care | Gloves + mask/eye protection if splashing possible |
| Injections/fingersticks | Gloves |
| Respiratory care/suctioning | Gloves + mask + eye protection + gown |

**PPE Provided Free:** Nitrile gloves, surgical masks, N95 respirators (fit-tested), goggles, face shields, gowns

### Sharps Safety

**NEVER:**
- Recap needles two-handed
- Bend or break needles
- Reach into sharps container

**ALWAYS:**
- Use safety-engineered devices
- Activate safety feature immediately
- Dispose immediately in puncture-proof container
- Replace container when 3/4 full

### Hepatitis B Vaccination
Offered free within 10 days of hire. Three-dose series (0, 1, 6 months). May decline but must sign declination form.

### Exposure Incident Procedure

**Immediate Actions:**
1. **Wash exposed area** - Needle sticks/cuts with soap and water; flush eyes/mucous membranes 15 minutes
2. **Report to supervisor immediately**
3. **Seek medical evaluation within 2 hours**
4. **Complete incident report**

**Follow-up:** Baseline testing immediately, repeat at 6 weeks, 3 months, 6 months

### Training Required
- **Initial:** Before patient contact (1-2 hours)
- **Annual:** Refresher every 12 months

## 6. VEHICLE & DRIVING SAFETY

### Driver Requirements
- Valid driver's license
- Clean driving record (no major violations past 3 years)
- Current auto insurance
- No DUI/DWI past 5 years

**License verified annually. Report violations within 5 days.**

### Cell Phone Policy - ZERO TOLERANCE

**PROHIBITED While Driving:**
- Texting or reading texts
- Email, social media, internet
- Handheld phone calls
- Taking photos/videos

**ALLOWED:**
- Hands-free calls (Bluetooth/speaker)
- Voice-activated GPS
- Emergency 911 (pull over if possible)

**If you need to use phone, PULL OVER SAFELY.**

**Violation may result in immediate termination.**

### Safe Driving Practices
- Obey all traffic laws and speed limits
- No eating, grooming, reading while driving
- Maintain 3-4 second following distance
- Adjust speed for weather
- Never drive fatigued or impaired
- Wear seat belt always (no exceptions)

### Accident Procedure
1. **Stop immediately** - Turn on hazards, check for injuries, call 911 if needed
2. **Call supervisor** - Notify immediately
3. **Document** - Exchange info, get witness contacts, take photos, get police report
4. **Seek medical care** if injured
5. **Complete incident report** within 24 hours

## 7. VIOLENCE PREVENTION & WORKPLACE SECURITY

### Zero Tolerance for:
- Physical assault or threats
- Verbal abuse or harassment
- Sexual harassment or assault
- Possession of weapons (unless authorized officer)
- Intimidating or threatening behavior

### Pre-Service Risk Assessment

Assess for:
- [ ] History of violence/aggression
- [ ] Dementia/confusion
- [ ] Substance abuse
- [ ] Weapons in home (secured properly?)
- [ ] Household members with concerning behaviors
- [ ] High-crime or isolated location

### Warning Signs of Escalating Behavior
- Raised voice, angry tone
- Aggressive posture, clenched fists
- Pacing, intense staring
- Verbal threats, throwing objects
- Invading personal space, blocking exit

### De-Escalation Techniques
1. **Stay calm** - Control voice and breathing
2. **Listen** - Acknowledge feelings
3. **Give space** - Maintain 6-8 feet distance
4. **Be respectful** - Calm language, no arguing
5. **Offer options** - Give sense of control
6. **Set limits** - State acceptable behavior calmly
7. **Seek help** - Call supervisor or 911

### When to Leave Immediately
- Threats of violence
- Weapon displayed
- Physical assault or attempt
- Exit blocked
- Situation escalating despite de-escalation
- **You feel in danger**

**Procedure:** Leave calmly but quickly → Safe location → Call 911 if needed → Call supervisor → Do not return

### Sexual Harassment Response
1. Clearly state: "That is not appropriate. Please stop."
2. If continues, terminate visit and leave
3. Report to supervisor same day

**You have right to refuse future visits. No retaliation for reporting.**

## 8. INJURY & ILLNESS REPORTING

### What to Report
**ALL** injuries, illnesses, exposures, near-misses, unsafe conditions, equipment malfunctions

**Even if:** Seems minor, don't need care, unsure if work-related

### Reporting Timeline

**IMMEDIATE (Same Day):**
- Any injury needing medical attention
- Blood/body fluid exposure
- Back injury, motor vehicle accident, violence
- Any incident where you feel injured

**Within 24 Hours:**
- Minor injuries (small cuts, bruises)
- Strains/soreness after work
- Near-miss incidents

**Critical:** Report within 30 days or workers comp claim may be denied per Texas law.

### How to Report
1. **Stop and assess** - Provide first aid, call 911 if serious
2. **Call supervisor immediately** - [Name] at [Phone]
3. **Complete incident report form** - Within 24 hours
4. **Seek medical care** - Go to designated workers comp provider

### Workers Compensation Benefits
**Medical Benefits:** All treatment covered, no co-pays

**Disability Benefits (if off work 7+ days):**
- Temporary Income Benefits (TIBs)
- 70% of average weekly wage
- Starts after 7-day waiting period

### Return-to-Work Program
Modified duty available when possible:
- Office work, training development, light tasks
- Allows you to earn wages while healing
- Gradual transition back to full duty

**Your Rights:**
- Report injuries without retaliation
- Receive medical care
- Workers comp benefits if eligible
- Safe workplace
- Refuse unsafe work

**Your Responsibilities:**
- Report all injuries promptly and honestly
- Follow medical advice and restrictions
- Attend all appointments
- Provide work status updates

1. **Leave immediately** if possible

3. Call 911 when safe
